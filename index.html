What is the purpose of this visualization?

This interactive map display all known nuclear tests that have happened since 1945. More informations and statistics about those tests are displayed as charts and contextual bubbles.

How does this work?

Press play or use the slider at the bottom to move through time. Nuclear tests are shown as circles on the map. The radius of the circle represents the magnitude of the explosion.

Click on the bubble button on the top left to display contextual information for milestone explosions.

The history button will pause the animation and show all events that happened until the currently selected date.

Nuclear testing over time - the histogram

The histogram above the slider displays the amount of nuclear events for each year. It gives an insight about the most active years in the nuclear's history.

Nuclear testing per country - the scoreboard

The scoreboard on the left panel shows the total number of nuclear tests carried out by each country up to the current date.

The data

The data comes from the Geoscience Agency of Australia.
